# World fiction — the Verdant Ruin

> Owned by `story-lead`. This file is the lore primer for Design (palette / mood) and Dev
> (audio direction). It is intentionally short. Nothing here is canon outside this repo;
> all names and motifs are original to the Wonder Boy Tribute project.

## One-paragraph pitch

Long after a quiet civilization stepped sideways out of the world, its terraces and
aqueducts went on living without it. Vines re-roofed the courtyards. Glass beetles nested
in the cisterns. A boy named **Reed Bramblestep** crosses these dawn-lit ruins on foot,
chasing a rumor — that his sister's voice was last heard somewhere past the moss-line —
and the ruins, half-asleep, shoo him along with whatever they have to throw.

## Tone & cadence

The Verdant Ruin is **gentle, not grim**. Threats here are *insistent* rather than cruel:
a thorned vine doesn't hate you, it simply grew where you wanted to stand. The mood the
soundtrack should chase is *kettle-warm morning* — flutes and small bells, water under
stone, the occasional far bird. Combat hits should land *clack-soft*, not crunchy.

Visually, the eye should rest on **layered greens cooled by river-blue and warmed by
dawn-amber**. Shadows are violet-grey, never black. Reed himself is the warmest object
on screen by design; the hero should read like a struck match against the foliage.

## What this means downstream

- **Design** — palette anchors: moss-green, river-stone-grey, dawn-amber, violet-shade.
  Avoid pure black ink lines; favor a one-step-darker hue from the local field.
- **Dev** — audio mood is woody/wet, not metallic. Hit sounds: short, tuned, untreble.
  Death sound is a *small inhale*, not a scream. No blood VFX — leaves, sparks, dust.

## What we are NOT writing

- No named gods, no prophecy, no overarching Big Bad. Phase 1 is the first morning of a
  long walk. Save mythology for Phases 3–4 if it earns its place.
- No callbacks to the inspirational original. New names, new silhouettes, new fauna.
